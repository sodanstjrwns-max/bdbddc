1What is Invisalign?

Invisalign® is a clear aligner orthodontic system developed by Align Technology (USA). It uses a series of custom-made transparent plastic trays (typically 20-50 aligners) — each worn for 1-2 weeks — to gradually move teeth into the desired position.

Unlike traditional metal braces, Invisalign is nearly invisible, removable, comfortable, and requires fewer office visits. Since FDA approval in 1999, over 18 million patients worldwide have been treated.

💡 In one sentence: Invisalign = "A series of 20-50 custom clear aligners designed in 3D — worn in sequence to move teeth into the planned final position."

7ClinCheck Workflow

ClinCheck is the heart of Invisalign treatment. The orthodontist uses Align's proprietary 3D software to design every single tooth movement, aligner-by-aligner.

What the Orthodontist Designs in ClinCheck

  • Each tooth's movement direction · distance · rotation angle (0.1mm precision)
  • Movement per aligner (typically 0.25mm; ±0.10mm with SmartTrack)
  • Attachment position · shape · count
  • IPR amount · location · timing
  • Total aligners needed · treatment duration
  • Final outcome (post-treatment alignment & bite)
💡 Patient benefit: Before starting treatment, you can preview the final result in 3D animation. Invisalign is the only orthodontic system with a "before/after preview & agreement" process.

8Attachments & IPR Explained

Attachments

Small tooth-colored composite resin bumps bonded to tooth surfaces. Aligners grip these to perform difficult movements.

  • Shapes: Rectangular, elliptical, beveled, optimized — ~8 types
  • Size: 2-5mm
  • Used for: Rotation (canines), extrusion, root movement, torque (angle)
  • Removal: Easily polished off at end of treatment — no enamel damage

IPR (Interproximal Reduction)

Removing up to 0.5mm between adjacent teeth to create space.

  • Amount: 0.2-0.5mm per site (within enamel — no sensitivity)
  • Tools: Special strips · thin diamond discs
  • Pain: None (enamel has no nerves)
  • Frequency: ~60% of Comprehensive cases require IPR

11Indications vs Limits

  • Open bite (mild to moderate)

  • Major arch expansion cases
  • Many implants/crowns — non-movable teeth handling
  • Active periodontal disease — treat first
  • Active TMD

13Extraction Cases

In the past, extraction cases (typically premolars) were considered unsuitable for Invisalign. With SmartTrack and attachment innovations, this is now possible — when performed by experienced Diamond-tier orthodontists.

Keys to Extraction-Case Success

  • Experienced Diamond+ orthodontist
  • High-quality ClinCheck design (preventing tooth tipping)
  • Power Ridges & optimized attachments
  • Expect 2-3 refinements
  • Treatment duration: typically 6 months longer than non-extraction

15Side Effects

Common

  • Mild discomfort 1-3 days after switching aligners (no NSAIDs needed)
  • Speech changes (first 1 week — adapts quickly)
  • Increased saliva (first day only)

Rare

  • Root resorption: 0.5-2% (lower than braces 3-5%)
  • Attachment debonding: 5-10% during treatment (easily re-bonded)
  • Gingivitis (if poor hygiene)
⚠️ Compliance is critical: 20-22 hours/day wear is mandatory. Less than 16 hours/day will cause treatment failure.

16Retainers — Prevent Relapse

Types

  • Vivera (Align): Clear aligners as retainers — quarterly replacement (1 set = 4 retainers, ~2 years use)
  • Hawley/Essix: Traditional clear plastic
  • Fixed (lingual bonded): Thin wire bonded behind front teeth — permanent retention

Schedule

  • Months 1-6: Full time (20-22 hours)
  • Months 6-12: Nighttime only (8-10 hours)
  • Years 1-3: Every other night
  • Year 3+: 2-3 nights/week — lifetime recommended

1935 Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Is Invisalign really invisible?

From 1 meter away, virtually undetectable. Attachments match tooth color. Popular among professionals, executives, public-facing roles.

Q2. How many hours per day must I wear them?

20-22 hours mandatory. Remove only for meals, brushing, and brief special occasions. Less than 16 hours/day = treatment failure.

Q3. Any food restrictions?

While wearing: water only. When removed: no restrictions. Unlike braces, you can eat anything (steak, popcorn, sticky candy).

Q4. How often do I change aligners?

Standard: 1-2 weeks per aligner. Modern protocols often use 7-10 day changes (faster but requires more compliance).

Q5. How often do I see the orthodontist?

Domestic patients: every 6-8 weeks. International patients (3-month visit interval): see protocol above.

Q6. Is Invisalign painful?

Mild pressure 1-3 days after switching to a new aligner. Most patients describe it as "tightness" rather than pain. Much less painful than braces.

Q7. Can adults use Invisalign?

Yes — 65% of Invisalign patients are adults. 30s, 40s, 50s common. No upper age limit if periodontal health is good (even 70s).

Q8. Are extractions needed?

Depends on case. Mild-moderate crowding can be solved with IPR. Severe crowding/skeletal issues may need premolar extraction. ClinCheck determines.

Q9. Are attachments visible?

Tooth-colored composite — barely visible in normal light. Up close (within 1cm), small bumps may be visible.

Q10. Does attachment placement hurt?

Completely painless. Etch + bond + light cure — 5-10 min. Removal is also painless, ~10 minutes.

Q11. Will my face shape change?

Mouth profile improves; E-line (nose-chin) becomes more harmonious. Especially noticeable in overbite cases.

Q12. Treatment time vs braces?

Equivalent — Comprehensive 12-24 months. With 7-day SmartTrack changes, 4 months faster than braces on average (Buschang 2014).

Q13. Can I do Invisalign while pregnant/nursing?

Yes — no drugs or X-rays needed during treatment continuation. New starts need initial X-rays — wait until second trimester.

Q14. What about implants/crowns?

Non-movable teeth excluded from planning. Teeth around implants can still be moved. Crown-heavy mouths need careful evaluation.

Q15. Do I need to remove wisdom teeth?

Not always required — but recommended for stability of treatment outcome. Fully impacted, asymptomatic teeth often left alone.

Q16. Can I whiten my teeth during treatment?

Yes! Aligners serve as bleaching trays — Invisalign + whitening combo available at Seoul BD Dental.

Q17. What if I stop treatment?

Teeth return toward original position. Resumable within 3 months with same aligners; after that, re-scan + redesign needed (additional fee).

Q18. What if I lose an aligner?

Go back to previous aligner and contact clinic. Replacement ordered from Align (~USD 100/aligner · 1 week). International shipping available.

Q19. Can I transfer care to my home country?

Possible but involves additional fees and re-planning. Since all aligners are pre-made, technically you can finish at home.

Q20. What's refinement?

Additional aligners if teeth don't move as planned. Comprehensive packages include 2-3 refinements free.

Q21. How long must I wear retainers?

Ideally lifetime nighttime wear. Teeth keep shifting with age. Months 1-6: full time, then nights only.

Q22. Are retainers included in cost?

Varies by clinic. Seoul BD Dental includes Vivera retainer set (4 retainers / ~2 years use). Additional sets ~USD 500.

Q23. Is iTero painful?

Completely painless. Optical sensor — no contact required. 5-10 minutes vs 30-60 min for silicon impressions. No gag reflex.

Q24. Bad breath concerns?

Since aligners are removable, oral hygiene is easier than with braces. Clean aligners daily with water + special cleaner.

Q25. How to clean aligners?

Soft toothbrush + water (cool or lukewarm) twice daily. Avoid toothpaste (abrasive). Weekly Invisalign Cleaning Crystals recommended.

Q26. Coffee, wine, smoking?

While wearing: water only. Aligners off → anything OK, but brush before re-inserting. Smoking strongly discouraged (stains aligners, gum disease).

Q27. Sports OK?

Yes — safer than braces. For contact sports, use a sports mouthguard over aligners (consult orthodontist).

Q28. Playing wind instruments?

Less impact than braces. 1-2 week adjustment period for serious musicians. Practice early before important performances.

Q29. Kissing?

Natural feel even while wearing. No metal sensation. Remove for special occasions (but minimize time off).

Q30. How is treatment completion determined?

Orthodontist evaluates final scan, photos, and bite check against ClinCheck final state. If incomplete → refinement.

Q31. Will I need re-treatment later?

With retainer compliance — <5% need re-treatment. Without retainers — significant relapse in 2-10 years.

Q32. Insurance coverage?

USA: Many dental insurance plans cover 50% up to lifetime max (~USD 1,500-3,000). HSA/FSA eligible. UK NHS: limited adult ortho coverage. Korea: not covered by Korean insurance for foreigners.

Q33. Good for kids/teens?

Yes. Invisalign First (6-10), Teen (11-16). Teen package includes compliance indicators and 6 free replacement aligners.

Q34. Can I just consult without committing?

Yes — free online consultation (email/WhatsApp with photos). No pressure to proceed. Review materials at home before deciding.
